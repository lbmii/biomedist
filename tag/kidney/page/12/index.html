RP105 protects against ischemic and septic acute kidney injury via suppressing TLR4/NF-κB signaling pathways
Highlights RP105 is a member of the TLR family and its expression plays a role in cardiovascular disease but the therapeutic impact on acute kidney injury remained unclear. We have uncovered evidence that RP105 expression in proximal tubular cells plays a kidney protective role during ischemic and septic acute kidney injury.

New mechanism of nephrotoxicity of triptolide: Oxidative stress promotes cGAS-STING signaling pathway
Highlights Triptolide activates the cGAS-STING signaling pathway in kidney tubular cells in vivo and in vitro. STING inhibitor C176 had protective effects in TPL-induced nephrocyte damage. Triptolide induced an enhancement in protein levels of BACH1, while the expression of HMOX1 was reduced.
