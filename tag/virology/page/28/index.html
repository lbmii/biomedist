The cGAS-STING pathway drives type I IFN immunopathology in COVID-19
Abstract COVID-19, which is caused by infection with SARS-CoV-2, is characterized by lung pathology and extrapulmonary complications. Type I interferons (IFNs) have an essential role in the pathogenesis of COVID-19. Although rapid induction of type I IFNs limits virus propagation, a sustained increase in the levels of type I IFNs in the late phase of the infection is associated with aberrant inflammation and poor clinical outcome.

Abstract Using online surveys, we collected data regarding COVID-19-related loss of smell or taste from 69,841 individuals. We performed a multi-ancestry genome-wide association study and identified a genome-wide significant locus in the vicinity of the UGT2A1 and UGT2A2 genes.
