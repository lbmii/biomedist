Maturation and specialization of group 2 innate lymphoid cells through the lung-gut axis
Abstract Innate lymphoid cells (ILC) are abundant in mucosal tissues. They serve critical functions in anti-pathogen response and tissue homeostasis. However, the heterogenous composition of ILCs in mucosal sites and their various maturation trajectories are less well known.
