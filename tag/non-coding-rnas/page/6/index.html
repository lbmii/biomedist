5-methylcytosine (m5C) RNA modification controls the innate immune response to virus infection by regulating type I interferons
Significance 5-methylcytosine (m5C) is a common RNA methylation. However, its biological functions remain a mystery. Here, we discover that depletion of NSUN2, an m5C methyltransferase, leads to an enhanced type I interferon (IFN) response, which significantly inhibits replication of a wide range of RNA and DNA viruses in vitro.
