E-cigarette vaping associated acute lung injury (EVALI): state of science and future research needs
Abstract “E-Cigarette (e-cig) Vaping-Associated Acute Lung Injury” (EVALI) has been linked to vitamin-E-acetate (VEA) and Δ-9-tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), due to their presence in patients’ e-cigs and biological samples. Lacking standardized methodologies for patients’ data collection and comprehensive physicochemical/toxicological studies using real-world-vapor exposures, very little data are available, thus the underlying pathophysiological mechanism of EVALI is still unknown.
