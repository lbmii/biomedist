Genome-wide Association Study in Acute Tubulointerstitial Nephritis

Thursday, 02 February 2023
Journal of the American Society of Nephrology PubMed

Abstract

Background: Acute tubulointerstitial nephritis (ATIN) is a rare immune related disease, accounting for about 10% of cases of unexplained acute kidney injury (AKI). Previous elucidation of the relationship between genetic factors that contribute to its pathogenesis was hampered due to small sample sizes and candidate gene design.

Methods: We undertook the first two-stage genome-wide association studies (GWAS) and meta-analysis involving 544 kidney biopsy-defined cases of ATIN and 2,346 controls with Chinese ancestry. We conducted statistical fine-mapping analysis, provided functional annotations of significant variants, estimated single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP)-based heritability, and checked genotype and sub-phenotype correlations.

Results: Two genome-wide significant loci, rs35087390 of HLA-DQA1 (P= 3.01×10-39) on 6p21.32 and rs2417771 of PLEKHA5 on 12p12.3 (P = 2.14×10-8), emerged from the analysis. HLA imputation using two reference panels suggested that HLA-DRB1-14 mainly drives the HLA risk association. HLA-DRB1 residue 60 belonging to pockets P10 was the key amino acid position. The SNP-based heritability estimates with and without the HLA locus were 20.43% and 10.35%. Different clinical sub-phenotypes (drug-related or tubulointerstitial nephritis and uveitis syndrome) seemed to share the same risk alleles. But HLA risk genotype was associated with disease severity and response rate to immunosuppressive therapy.

Conclusions: We identified two candidate genome regions associated with susceptibility to ATIN. The findings suggest that a genetically conferred risk of immune dysregulation is involved in the pathogenesis of ATIN.
